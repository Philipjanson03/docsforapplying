## ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Factors & Contingency Plans

### **Risk 1: IELTS score too low**
**Contingency:**
- Take exam in early March (allows retake in April if needed)
- Have backup: TOEFL (some students find it easier)
- Bologna accepts 5.5 (lower threshold)

### **Risk 2: Application deadline missed**
**Contingency:**
- Pisa has longest window (until June 30)
- Keep backup university with later deadline
- Some universities have second intake calls

### **Risk 3: Visa delay**
**Contingency:**
- Apply for visa immediately in June (don't wait)
- Have all documents perfect (reduces processing time)
- Consider requesting expedited processing
- Contact university about late arrival policies

### **Risk 4: No admission/rejection**
**Contingency:**
- Apply to 5+ universities (diversify)
- Include mix of competitive + achievable
- Consider Fall 2027 intake (gap year can be positive)

### **Risk 5: Financial shortage**
**Contingency:**
- Apply to ALL scholarship opportunities
- Choose more affordable universities (Sapienza, Pisa)
- Consider part-time work in Italy (20h/week allowed)
- Family support planning

## üí° Pro Tips

**Time Management:**
- Set phone reminders for ALL deadlines (1 week before, 3 days before, 1 day before)
- Use Google Calendar with notifications
- Check email 2x daily during application period
- Keep all confirmations in dedicated folder

**Document Organization:**
- Create cloud backup (Google Drive/Dropbox) of ALL documents
- Keep physical copies + digital copies
- Organize by university name
- Name files clearly: "LastName_FirstName_Document_UniversityName.pdf"

**Communication:**
- Email universities in English (they have international offices)
- Be polite, concise, specific in emails
- Save all correspondence
- Response time: usually 3-7 business days

**Mental Health:**
- This is stressful - that's normal!
- Take breaks during application season
- Don't compare to others
- Celebrate small wins (IELTS score, application submitted, etc.)
- Have support system (friends/family)
