### 4. IELTS Academic Certificate

## ‚úçÔ∏è Written Application Materials

### 8. Curriculum Vitae (CV)
- [ ] **European format** (Europass template)
- [ ] **English language**
- [ ] **Updated** (most recent)
- [ ] **Tailored** to AI/ML field
- [ ] **1-2 pages** (maximum)

**Must Include:**
- Personal information
- Education history
- Work experience (if any)
- Skills (programming languages, tools, frameworks)
- Projects (AI/ML related)
- Publications (if any)
- Languages (Farsi native, English, others)
- Awards/achievements

**Template:** Download Europass CV template
**Format:** PDF

---

### 9. Motivation Letter / Statement of Purpose
- [ ] **Unique for each university** (don't use same one!)
- [ ] **1-2 pages** (usually 500-1000 words)
- [ ] **English**
- [ ] **Proofread** by native speaker if possible

**Structure:**
1. Introduction (who you are, what you're applying for)
2. Academic background (relevant coursework, projects)
3. Why this specific program (research interests)
4. Why this specific university (professors, facilities)
5. Career goals (how master's fits your plan)
6. Why you're a good fit (skills, experience, passion)
7. Conclusion

**Tips:**
- Be specific (mention professors, labs, research groups)
- Show genuine interest
- Connect your background to future goals
- Professional but personal tone
- No grammar mistakes!

**Format:** PDF
**Track:** Create separate file for each university

---

### 10. Letters of Recommendation (2-3)
- [ ] **Identified 2-3 professors** at IAUT
- [ ] **Asked professors** (give 1 month notice)
- [ ] **Provided information** (program details, your goals)
- [ ] **Received letters**
- [ ] **Sealed envelopes** (signed across seal) OR
- [ ] **Direct submission** (professors email to university)

**Who to ask:**
- Professors who taught you core CS/AI courses
- Thesis supervisor (if you have one)
- Lab instructors who know your work
- Choose those who can speak to your abilities

**Content should cover:**
- Your academic performance
- Research potential
- Relevant skills
- Work ethic
- Why you're suitable for graduate studies

**Format:** University letterhead, signed, PDF
**Language:** English

## üáÆüáπ Italy-Specific Documents

### 13. Declaration of Value (Dichiarazione di Valore)
- [ ] **Apply through Italian Embassy** in Tehran
- [ ] **Submit degree + transcripts**
- [ ] **Wait for processing** (can take weeks)
- [ ] **Receive official document**

**What it is:** Italian Embassy certification that your Iranian degree is legitimate and equivalent
**When needed:** Usually during enrollment, sometimes for application
**Processing time:** 4-8 weeks (apply early!)
**Cost:** Check with embassy

---

### 14. Universitaly Registration
- [ ] **Create account** on https://www.universitaly.it/
- [ ] **Upload documents**
- [ ] **Pre-enrollment completed** (by April 29, 2026)
- [ ] **Confirmation received**

**Critical:** ALL non-EU students MUST register here for visa
**Deadline:** April 29, 2026 (for Fall 2026)

## üí° Pro Tips

**Translation:**
- Use official/certified translator (check embassy list)
- Keep originals safe
- Make multiple copies of translations
- Some docs may need notarization

**Scans:**
- Use 300 DPI minimum
- Color scans for passport, photos
- Clear and readable
- PDF format (not JPG for documents)

**Certified Copies:**
- Some universities want "certified true copies"
- Get notarized if required
- Check specific requirements

**Timeline:**
- Start document collection EARLY
- Some processes take weeks
- Plan for delays
- Have buffer time

## ‚ùì Common Questions

**Q: Do I need original documents or copies?**
A: Usually digital copies for online application, originals for in-person enrollment

**Q: All translations must be official?**
A: Yes, especially for degree, transcripts, and visa documents

**Q: Can I use electronic signatures?**
A: Check specific requirements; often physical signatures needed

**Q: What if IAUT doesn't provide some documents?**
A: Contact university's international office to explain and ask for alternatives

**Q: How many recommendation letters?**
A: Usually 2 minimum, 3 maximum. Check each university's requirements.
